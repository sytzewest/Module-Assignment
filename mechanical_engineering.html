Mechanical Engineering

BSc Mechanical Engineering, 4 years, Full time

About the course

As a mechanical engineer, you will be involved with devising, designing, fabricating and improving equipment, tools, appliances, machines and factories. All things that make our lives – in business and as consumers – easier, safer and more sustainable. You will use your knowledge of mathematics and physics to look at the world from an analytical perspective. You will learn to critically assess obvious solutions and devise better alternatives, making you an indispensable link between technological development and society.

A mechanical engineer not only has knowledge of mathematics and physics, but also of lifespan, energy consumption, safety and recycling. Technical systems and processes are becoming increasingly advanced and complex and can be found in a variety of places. As a result, mechanical engineers are employed throughout society, primarily in technology, both for large multinationals such as Unilever and Heineken and for smaller engineering firms. But there is also a demand for mechanical engineers in government bodies and education.
