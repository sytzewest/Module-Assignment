Job Prospects

A Bachelor's in aerospace engineering will give you the credentials to work for a multinational firm, governmental institutions and international organisations. Because all our programmes are EQUIS and AACSB accredited, a standard which only one percent of universities reach worldwide, your degree will be highly valued on the labour market. If you were to choose the Research Master's you could move into academia, perhaps decide to go further and earn a PhD.

Plenty of multinationals will be interested in students with an academic degree in aerospace engineering. For instance companies like Boeing, Airbus and Rolls Royce all need internationally trained employees. Your position within these kind of organisations can vary from Senior Technical Engineer or Consultant Engineer, to Technical Team Manager. But numerous other career opportunities will lie ahead of you. This of course depends on the choices you make during and after studying. Many organisations also offer internships and junior positions which will introduce you to different types of opportunities.

Job Examples

  • Consultant Engineer
  • Technical Team Manager
