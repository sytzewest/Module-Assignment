Studying with Disability

Studying with a disability may require additional time and effort. Find out what support is available at Osprey University and consider what might be helpful in your situation.

A disability is an impairment that may be cognitive, developmental, intellectual, mental, physical, sensory, or some combination of these. For example:

-a visual, auditory or physical disability;

-a language disorder (for example dyslexia) or speech disorder;

-a chronic or psychological illness.

Facilities

Studying with a disability may require additional time and effort. Adaptations can be made to the ways in which you follow classes and take exams. Provisions such as audio-visual aids for lectures and lifts can also be made available.

Special arrangements and facilities offered by Osprey University include:


  • Access ways for people with physical disabilities;
  • Extra time for homework and tests;
  • The aid of an interpreter for poeple with auditory disabilities;
  • Partial exemptions from tutorial attendance requirements;
  • Recording lectures/tutorials;
  • Having an alternative type of examination;
